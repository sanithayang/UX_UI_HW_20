Overview
The goal of this mobile app is to bring a one stop shop to consumers by creating a virtual shopping experience where they can explore, try-on, and buy from anywhere at any time. This provides convenience and inclusivity considering the pandemic having changed the way consumers shop for clothes.
Role
Role: UX Designer
Timeline: 2 1/2 weeks
Tools: Figma, Figjam
Team: Sanitha Yang, Taylor Evans, Kassia Kopp & Assata Kokayi
Approach
User Research (interviews, data analysis)
Brainstorm and Ideation
Wireframing and Prototyping
User Testing
Results
PerfectFIT offers a safe, accessible, inclusive environment for people to explore their authentic style within the comfort of their own space. Where fashion, value and technology meet to bring you the perfect fit every time!

Methodology

In this research project, we planned and conducted 5 user interviews to discover a user’s confidence and frustrations when shopping online compared to in-store shopping.

Objectives
  • What factors determine a user’s online purchase decision (quality, size, style, price, etc.)?
  • How to improve the user’s experience using a virtual shopping platform to fulfill their style goals and increase their confidence in online purchases.
Trends
  • Virtual shopping would be convenient
  • Saves time for travel
  • Frustration with finding quality clothing

User Research & Findings

User Insight

Online shoppers are open to virtual shopping and are looking for an efficient way to find an array of quality items at affordable prices in safer environments.

Problem Statement

Online shoppers are conflicted when making purchases and need an efficient way to find quality clothing in the comfort and privacy of their homes.

Ideation Design

User Research: Key Takeaway

The ability to try on clothes virtually at any time in the comfort of the user’s home and being able to see how various styles of clothing would fit their body type were high on the list.

Key Feature to Ideate on

Virtual closet feature which would allow users to virtually try on different outfits.

Usability Testing & Findings

Objective

Test the onboarding flow to see if users can sign up and successfully create their closet.

Tasks
  1. Sign up with E-mail or connect with a social media network
  2. Create Closet
  3. Login
User Feedback
  • Give users the option to multi-select when answering questions
  • Incorporate a visual of a closet when opening the fitting room to be more interactive
  • Ensure the flow has no dead ends for the convenience of the user
  • Include Instagram as a social sign-in option (and keep social media options)

Conclusions

Takeaways
  • Communicating different suggestions among team members brought valuable insight and an even greater product by combining ideas
  • Occasionally the team would focus on other features/ideas that would sometimes take up time and would have to remind ourselves about the project deadline and constraints - we had to learn how to prioritize based on our user research
  • Empathy is a crucial part of design thinking!
Future Iterations
  • Add “What are you shopping for?’ as the first thing users see when they log in
  • Add a beauty aspect to the app
  • Connect social media with the virtual closet
  • Express that the app is gender inclusive
